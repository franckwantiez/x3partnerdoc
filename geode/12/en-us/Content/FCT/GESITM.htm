Products

A product is characterized by a unique identifier by depositor. The information in the product record is used by a large number of processes and it therefore advisable to take care when creating and maintaining it.

This function allows the user to maintain the product table and its containers. It allows the user to create, cancel, update and view the database information associated with a product.

There are 2 major types of products managed in Warehousing:

CU-managed products (CU for Consumption Unit)

Setup (Tab Controls): Flag "Stock Unit management" not checked (default value) and Stocking unit checked or not

A CU-managed product is characterized by the fact that its stock is CU-managed All the flows (input/output) are managed by packing from the pallet (level 1 to the CU (level 5).
The stock is viewed by number of CUs. An option makes it possible to define a stock unit (SU) and thus view the stock in stock unit.

Example: CU product without stock unit: the screwdriver
the CU is thus defined, as well as the box pack of 5 CUs, the box pallet, etc.
the stock is recorded in CUs

Example: CU product with stock unit "Litre": the milk bottle
 the CU is defined (the 1-litre milk bottle), the 6-bottle pack is defined, etc.
the stock is recorded in CUs but also in litres.

SU-managed product (SU for Stock Unit)

Setup (Tab Controls): Flag "Stock unit management" checked and Stocking unit entered

A SU-managed product is characterized by the fact that its stock is SU-managed andand decimalized. All the flows (input/output) are managed by packing from the pallet (level 1 to the SU (level 5).
The stock is viewed by number of SUs.

Example: SU product (SU = the meter): the cloth roll
the SU is defined, a well as the 100-meter cloth roll and the 6-roll pallet, etc.
the stock is recorded in SUs.

 The SU-managed products are associated with the following functional limitations:

  • At general level:
    • Entry format of the serial numbers
    • Cross-docking is not managed.
    • A SU product cannot be used as a compound or a kit component.
    • Dynamic picking is not managed (mapping module).
  • At input level:
    • There is no incomplete container equivalence, nor any container equivalence for new "unit" containers (level 5) 
  • At output level:
    • Not pivot picking management
    • Not multiple picking management
  • A stock level:
    • No assembly management

Tab Checks

CU and SU managed products

The type of the product at bay is entered in this tab:

  • CU management:
    • Flag "Stock Unit management" not checked (default value)
    • "Stock unit" (optional): entered if the SU stock needs to be known. In that case, the SU quantity represented by the CU needs to be defined (to be defined on the product container of level 5, the CU).
  • SU management:
    • Flag "Stock Unit management" checked
    • "Stock unit" (mandatory): entered. In that case, the product container of level 5 will represent the SU.

Comments on the duration management

1- Stock having a Best Before Date not expired is not considered in the automatic output processes.

2- Stock having an expired SbD (Sale by Date), UBD (Use By Date) or SBD (Send By Date) is not considered in the automatic output processes. It is considered as being blocked.

3- The values above can be expressed in number of days or number of months.

4- If the duration is not mentioned at product record level, but it is expressed in "months" or "days", by default, the entity is equal to the current date; if the FIFO date is equal to a date whose duration is not mentioned, then it becomes mandatory upon entry.

Tab Containers

  This tab displays the palletization program for the product, defined by clicking the Containers button. The only update to take place on this screen is the definition of the default container.

Cnt. The container type and code represents the packing type of a product and it mentions the defined levels. For each item, the breakdown of the palletization program(s) where the item can be described, must be known to the system.

A palletization program can be broken down into 5 levels of containers (linked to the Type) which are hierarchically nested (principle of the Russian nesting dolls).

Several palletization programs can be defined, among which one is defined as the default palletization program, each of the programs corresponding to a physical packing.

A unique level-5 container must absolutely be defined for each product. Furthermore, based on the product type (CU or SU managed product), the level 5 container will be the CU or the SU.

Any product container, except for the level 5 container, must be described based on the container that it directly contains (lower container). The lower container has a container level higher than the described container.

Dft. This flag is triggered via a double-click. A single default container can be triggered per product. It is used for the input processes (display of the container upon creation of an input line, etc.) and output processes (authorized incomplete container, etc.).

Level. The container level represents the space that a container of this type should occupy in the palletization program of an item.

No. and Inf cnt. Number of containers inferior to the product container.

CU nb. Information automatically calculated by the system based on the data entered for the previous setup. (only displayed in case of a CU managed product)

SU quantity. Information automatically calculated by the system based on the data entered previously. (displayed in case of SU managed product or of CU managed product with SU).

SU. it features the stock unitof the product (displayed in case of SU managed product or of CU managed product with SU).

Input. This specifies if inputs to stock of the container for this product are authorized.

Output. It specifies if ouputs from stock of the container for this product are authorized.

Chaining. Code created in the Store Sequence Table. It defines the priority input store for the product container considered. The priority input store intervenes in the input addressing algorithm.

The provides direct access to the creation/modification of a container.
